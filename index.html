üèÉ Advanced Movement Techniques

Core Movement Mechanics

Sprint Boost (Weapon Holster)

Press 5 to holster all weapons. You run SIGNIFICANTLY faster with empty hands - use this for crossing open ground or escaping dangerous situations. This is your #1 movement tech for survival.

Dodge Roll

Press Left Alt to perform a dodge roll. Low i-frames but excellent for repositioning during combat and traveling across open areas. Use in combination with sprinting for maximum distance coverage.

Shoulder Swap

Press X to swap which shoulder you're aiming over. CRITICAL for corner work and staircase navigation. Different cover situations require different shoulder positions. Master this for consistent wins in third-person angle battles.

Advanced Combat Movement

Slide ‚Üí Shoot ‚Üí Roll ‚Üí Shoot Combo

The ultimate aggressive CQB technique. Perform a slide, shoot during slide, immediately roll on landing, then shoot again. This breaks enemy tracking, maintains pressure, and maximizes burst damage in 1v1 engagements.

Roll-Shoot Rhythm

Each weapon has its own movement rhythm. Learn to time your dodge rolls between shots to maintain DPS while staying mobile. High-risk but essential for confident aimers.

Constantly reposition during ARC combat. ARCs struggle with leading moving targets, so never remain stationary during active combat. Use cover aggressively and dodge roll frequently.

Pro Movement Tips

  • Stamina Management: Never let stamina fall to zero in open areas. Crouch or walk to restore stamina. If stamina depletes during combat, you're dead.
  • Ladder Speed: Advanced tech exists for climbing ladders 3x faster - look up/spam jump while climbing
  • Zero Fall Damage: Dodge roll right before landing from any height to negate ALL fall damage
  • Elevation Advantage: Always seek high ground. Better visibility and concealment from towers, rooftops, and ledges
  • Sound Discipline: Only sprint when necessary. Crouch-walk near enemies. Sprinting, looting, and reloading all make noise
ARCs have a narrow 45-degree vision cone. Use stealth and positioning to avoid detection. Blue = passive patrol, Yellow = suspicious, Red = active engagement.

‚öîÔ∏è Combat Meta & PvP Tactics

Third-Person Angle Mastery

Third-person vision is your strongest mechanic. Learn to manipulate camera angles and you'll consistently get first-shot advantage. Peek corners with your camera, not your body.
  • Corner Peeking: Use shoulder swap (X) to peek from the correct side. Never expose more of your body than necessary
  • Cover Usage: Peek, shoot, duck, reposition. Never stay in the same spot after firing
  • Height Advantage: Control high ground to see over cover and force enemies into bad angles

PvP Combat Principles

Third-Partying

When you hear gunfire nearby, PUSH IMMEDIATELY. Don't allow enemies to heal or reset. If someone is 1-2 shots from dying, finish them before they recover. Even hitting an exposed arm counts - damage is damage.

Sound Cues = Free Intel

Master these sounds: footsteps, healing, shield charging, reloading, door movement. Skilled players rely on sound MORE than vision. Push aggressively when enemies are distracted or healing.

Never Trust "Friendly"

Many players say "friendly" just to get close and backstab you. NEVER lower your guard. NEVER show your back. ALWAYS keep crosshair ready. Assume betrayal until proven otherwise.

Loadout Strategy

  • Solo Players: Carry only ONE primary weapon. Save weight for healing and repositioning tools
  • Duo/Squad: One long-range, one close-range to cover all engagement distances
  • Ammo Types: Heavy ammo shreds both ARCs and players (best armor penetration). Medium is middle ground. Light is fast but weaker penetration
Free loadouts lead to passive gameplay - corner camping and third-party cleanup. Invest in real loadouts to play aggressively and control fights.

Combat Awareness

  • Track extraction timer - never get caught extracting during final minutes
  • Monitor sound radius - gunfire alerts everyone nearby
  • Choose regions based on goal: hot zones for PvP practice, cold zones for safe farming
  • Mind your sound: avoid sprinting, slamming doors, stepping on glass unless safe
  • Disengage when necessary - Arc Raiders isn't purely competitive, survival matters more
Deaths happen FAST in Arc Raiders. Adopt a defensive playstyle. Use cover religiously. Mind your sound. Disengage from unfavorable fights.

Special Mod: Anvil Splitter (legendary) - fires 3 projectiles, turns it into a sawed-off shotgun

Weapon Selection Strategy

Arc Raiders doesn't have "best in slot" - it's about matching weapons to your mission. ARC farmers need high penetration (Ferro, Anvil, Hullcracker). Versatile raiders want all-purpose weapons (Tempest, Rattler, Anvil). PvP specialists need medium/light ammo with high TTK.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overvaluing Rarity: The uncommon Anvil outperforms many epic weapons. Don't sleep on lower rarities.
  • Ignoring Weight: Heavy loadouts drain stamina and reduce loot capacity. Balance firepower with mobility.
  • Single-Purpose Loadouts: Bringing only ARC weapons leaves you vulnerable to players. Always have player-combat backup.
  • Neglecting Upgrades: Even common weapons become deadly with proper attachments. Upgrade your favorites.
  • Fighting Unfavorable Matchups: Don't waste light ammo on heavily armored ARCs.

ü§ñ ARC Enemy Combat Guide

Core ARC Combat Principles

ARCs are surprisingly blind. Every enemy operates with a narrow 45-degree vision cone. They can't hear you unless you're practically stepping on them (except Snitches). Movement is your best defense - ARCs struggle with leading targets.

Understanding ARC Armor

  • White Surfaces: Soft, unarmored - take FULL damage
  • Grey Plating: Heavy armor - significantly reduces damage unless using armor-piercing rounds
  • Yellow Components: WEAK SPOTS - deal maximum damage and disable critical systems. Always target these first!

Enemy Breakdown

Wasps (Flying Scout)

Threat: Low | Strategy: Focus fire on thrusters. Crash immediately after losing two rotors. Easiest aerial target.

Ticks

Threat: Low | Strategy: Small, fast melee units. Target weak spots, use doorways to limit approach angles.

Snitch (Surveillance Drone)

Threat: HIGH | Strategy: Enhanced audio detection. WILL CALL REINFORCEMENTS if it spots you. Priority target - take it out silently.

Sentinels

Threat: Medium | Strategy: Mobile turrets. Target yellow neck components. Predictable patrol patterns - use stealth.

Sniper Turrets

Threat: High | Strategy: Broadcast targeting with visible laser beams. Hit yellow neck components while staying outside firing arc. They only target body - expose just head and weapon.

Leapers

Threat: High | Strategy: Requires constant movement and precise timing. When it jumps, move SIDEWAYS - it cannot change direction mid-leap. Target glowing eye with sustained fire. Use stun effects for damage windows.

Rocketeers

Threat: Very High | Strategy: Heavy firepower, high health. Target leg joints and rear cylinders. Maintain cover at all times. These can shred you quickly.

Bastions (8-Legged)

Threat: Extreme | Strategy: Massive nightmares with variants:

  • Artillery Bastions: Eliminate spotter drones FIRST - main gun loses targeting without them
  • Standard Bastions: Move slowly but devastating firepower. Target leg joints and rear cylinders while maintaining cover
Queen / Matriarch

Threat: BOSS TIER | Strategy: Requires specialized loadouts. High armor penetration weapons essential. Team coordination recommended. Massive XP and loot rewards.

Pro ARC Combat Tips

For groups of small drones, position in doorways or narrow passages that limit approach angles and force predictable flight paths. Never fight in open areas.
  • ARCs are predictable - learn their patterns and exploit weaknesses
  • Always target yellow weak spots on rear sections, joints, exposed cores
  • Use armor-piercing rounds (heavy ammo) for heavily armored units
  • Maintain tactical positioning - never stay stationary during combat
  • Break line of sight to reset aggro on difficult encounters

‚öôÔ∏è Optimal Performance Settings

Display Settings

Window Mode Borderless Fullscreen

Best balance of performance and usability. Avoid Exclusive Fullscreen.

Resolution Native (1920x1080 / 2560x1440)

Always match your display's native resolution. Use AI upscaler if you need performance, NOT lower resolution.

V-Sync OFF

CRITICAL: Forces GPU to wait for monitor refresh. Adds MASSIVE input lag and ruins responsiveness.

Frame Rate Limit Slightly Above Average FPS

Cap at ~10 frames above your average to prevent stuttering. Don't push PC beyond capabilities.

FOV (Field of View) MAX (80-90)

MAXIMUM FOV gives best chance of spotting enemies and loot. Essential for competitive play.

Graphics Settings (Competitive)

Texture Quality HIGH

Minimal performance impact. Ensures clear material/surface distinction.

View Distance HIGH (not Ultra)

Need to spot enemies at long range, but Ultra renders unnecessary detail that hurts FPS.

Shadows LOW to MEDIUM

Shadows are expensive. Low gives huge FPS boost with minimal visual loss.

Effects Quality MEDIUM

You still see muzzle flashes, explosions, and critical combat effects. Major FPS gains.

Post-Processing LOW

Reduces screen clutter and improves visibility.

Foliage LOW

COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE: Low foliage = better visibility through grass/bushes. Enemies can't hide as easily.

Motion Blur OFF

DISABLE COMPLETELY. Severely obscures targets while tracking and turning. Zero competitive value.

Depth of Field OFF

Blurs background when ADS. Can obscure flanking enemies in peripheral vision.

Screen Shake OFF

Disrupts aim during explosions and sprinting. Makes tracking impossible.

Ray Tracing OFF (unless high-end GPU)

Most expensive setting in game. Zero competitive value. Kills FPS on all hardware.

AI Upscaling (CRITICAL for FPS)

DLSS (NVIDIA) Balanced or Quality

Renders at lower internal resolution then uses AI to upscale. INSTANT FPS boost without much visual loss.

FSR (AMD) Quality Mode

AMD's upscaling solution. Use if you don't have NVIDIA GPU.

Frame Generation DISABLED

Generates fake frames between real ones. Increases displayed FPS but adds SIGNIFICANT input latency. Only use on controller.

Advanced Performance Settings

NVIDIA Reflex ON + BOOST

CRITICAL: Lowest possible system latency. Optimizes render queue for CPU/GPU sync. More responsive controls and faster reaction to inputs.

AMD Anti-Lag ON

AMD's version of Reflex. Essential for reducing input delay.

System Optimization

Arc Raiders is EXTREMELY CPU-intensive in multiplayer. CPU bottlenecks are more common than GPU bottlenecks. Prioritize CPUs with strong single-thread performance (Ryzen 7800X3D/9800X3D or Intel i7-13700K/i9-14900K).
  • Close Background Apps: Chrome, Discord overlays, streaming software eat CPU/RAM. Close before playing.
  • Update Graphics Drivers: NVIDIA and AMD have Arc Raiders-optimized drivers. 5-10 FPS boost in intense spots.
  • Windows Power Plan: Set to "High Performance" to prevent CPU throttling during combat.
  • Disable Fullscreen Optimizations: Right-click .exe ‚Üí Properties ‚Üí Compatibility ‚Üí Check "Disable fullscreen optimizations"
  • Run from SSD: Shortens loading times and prevents texture pop-ins

Accessibility (Competitive Edge)

Crosshair Customization

Color: Super vibrant (bright pink/cyan) - rarely appears in environment

Outline: Enable with black or white outline for additional clarity

Default white crosshair is difficult to see. Custom crosshair significantly improves aim consistency.

If you're CPU bottlenecked (100% CPU usage, GPU at 40%), no amount of graphics settings will help. You need to upgrade your CPU or close background processes.

üìà Fast Leveling & XP Farming Guide

Max Level & XP System

Max level is 75 (75 skill points total). Reaching max level takes approximately 250 hours of active gameplay. Plan your skill tree carefully - you can't fill everything.

XP Sources Breakdown

Scavenging (MOST IMPORTANT)

XP: 200-500 XP per container | Strategy: THE #1 XP source. You get XP just for INTERACTING with a container - doesn't matter if it's empty or if teammate already looted it!

PRO EXPLOIT: In squads, have one teammate loot everything, then you "search" all the empty containers. You BOTH get full XP. This is the meta.

Time on Surface

XP: ~180 XP per minute | Strategy: You get paid just for BEING topside. Even terrible runs where you get no kills = you still get all time-survived XP. Stay alive as long as safely possible.

Safe Return Bonus

XP: 1,000-2,000 XP | Strategy: MASSIVE bonus for successful extraction. A safe 15-minute run that extracts is often worth MORE than a 29-minute high-kill run that ends in death. Don't get greedy!

Looting Dead Bodies

XP: 500 XP per Raider corpse, up to 2,000 XP per heavy ARC | Strategy: Big machines (Rocketeers, Bastions) break into multiple lootable pieces. Each piece = XP. Absolute XP pi√±atas.

Damaged ARC

XP: Varies by damage dealt | Strategy: You get XP for damage dealt + kill. Big beefy ARCs have huge health pools = thousands of XP just from damage.

Killed Raider (PvP)

XP: 500 XP per kill, 250 XP per assist | Strategy: High-risk, high-reward. Tap enemies your teammates are fighting for free assists. Scavenging is more reliable though.

Quests

XP: 5,000-30,000 XP | Strategy: Mid/late-game quests give MASSIVE XP bursts. Complete them alongside your normal looting for max efficiency.

Weekly Trials (Unlocks at Level 15)

XP: Variable + exclusive rewards | Strategy: Competitive ranking system with special challenges. Great for endgame XP.

Optimal XP Farming Strategy

BE A LOOT GOBLIN

This is BY FAR the most consistent XP method. Forget being a PvP god - obsessively search EVERY container. Even empty ones. This is your bread and butter.

Expected: 5,000-10,000 XP per safe run just from scavenging

SURVIVE, DON'T THRIVE

Long survival time = massive XP. A safe 20-minute run extracting with decent loot beats a risky 30-minute run ending in death. Prioritize survival over kills.

ALWAYS EXTRACT

That 1,000-2,000 XP extraction bonus is your real paycheck. Don't get greedy at the end. Extract safely even if you could loot more.

FARM ARCS, NOT PLAYERS

ARCs are predictable and give reliable XP (damage + kill + lootable corpse pieces). Players are unpredictable and might third-party you. Farm machines for consistent XP.

COMPLETE QUESTS PASSIVELY

Do quests WHILE looting and surviving. Don't go out of your way for dangerous objectives. Let them complete naturally during normal runs.

Typical XP Per Run

  • Safe Loot Run (15-20 min): 5,000-10,000 XP
  • High-Efficiency Run (with scavenging + ARCs): 15,000-20,000 XP
  • Quest Completion Run: 25,000-40,000 XP
  • Average XP/Hour (3-4 successful extractions): 15,000-40,000 XP
Consistency beats high-skill plays. Loot, shoot, scoot. The game rewards CONSISTENCY, not just PvP dominance. You can reach max level by being a cautious loot goblin who always extracts.

Leveling Priorities by Phase

Early Game (Levels 1-15)

  • Focus on quests for burst XP
  • Learn safe looting routes on Dam map
  • Avoid risky PvP - you need consistent extractions
  • Prioritize survival over loot quality

Mid Game (Levels 15-40)

  • Unlock Weekly Trials for bonus XP
  • Start farming heavy ARCs for big XP
  • Optimize looting routes for 15-20 min runs
  • Mix safe farming with quest objectives

Late Game (Levels 40-75)

  • XP requirement stabilizes at ~49,000 XP per level
  • Focus on high-value targets (Queens, Matriarchs)
  • Maximize efficiency with optimized routes
  • Consider Expedition (prestige) at 75
After Level 50, XP required per level stays at ~49,000 XP. The grind slows down significantly. Focus on efficient 15-20k XP runs rather than risking everything for 40k runs.
